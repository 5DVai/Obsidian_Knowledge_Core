## Key Concepts & Principles
- **Product Requirements Document (PRD):** A dynamic specification providing context and constraints for AI systems.
- **Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs):** Architectural constraints that guide system performance and optimization.
- **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG):** A method for providing context to AI systems using external knowledge bases.
- **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL):** A model where humans oversee and validate AI-generated outputs.
- **Gherkin Format:** A structured language for writing acceptance criteria in a machine-readable format.
- **Multi-Agent Systems (MASs):** Collaborative frameworks where specialized agents perform complex tasks autonomously.

## Detailed Technical Analysis

### The Evolution of PRDs
- **Traditional vs. AI-Driven PRDs:** Traditional PRDs are narrative documents, while AI-driven PRDs are structured specifications that serve as master prompts for AI systems.
- **Role in AI Systems:** PRDs provide the necessary context for AI agents to generate and validate code, ensuring alignment with business goals.

### Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)
- **Importance in AI Models:** NFRs become runtime directives for optimization agents, ensuring system performance and reliability.
- **FURPS+ Model:** A framework for categorizing NFRs into Functionality, Usability, Reliability, Performance, and Supportability.

### AI-Driven Workflows
- **Agent-Assisted vs. Agent-Driven:** Agent-assisted workflows augment human processes, while agent-driven workflows automate the entire development lifecycle.
- **Role of LLMs and MASs:** LLMs and MASs automate requirements engineering, code generation, and validation, reducing human intervention.

### Gherkin and Acceptance Criteria
- **Machine-Readable Contracts:** Gherkin format provides a formal specification for AI agents to generate and validate code against acceptance criteria.
- **Pro-Tip:** Use Gherkin for all acceptance criteria to ensure clarity and testability.
