## Enterprise Q&A Bank

1. **Q:** How can we ensure alignment between software initiatives and business strategy?
   **A:** Define business objectives and align them with the portfolio strategy, involving executives and product managers in the process.

2. **Q:** What steps should be taken to validate technical feasibility?
   **A:** Conduct feasibility assessments, review architectural options, and ensure alignment with regulatory and security contexts.

3. **Q:** How do we build a defensible business case for a new software project?
   **A:** Estimate market size, revenue model, costs, and ROI, while identifying key risks and ensuring alignment with governance boards.

4. **Q:** What are the key considerations for developing a robust solution architecture?
   **A:** Define target architecture, select appropriate patterns, confirm technology stack choices, and document security and scalability requirements.

5. **Q:** How can we ensure high-quality code delivery?
   **A:** Set up version control, configure CI/CD pipelines, develop features iteratively with automated tests, and apply secure coding standards.

6. **Q:** What is the role of non-functional testing in software delivery?
   **A:** Validate security, performance, scalability, and usability through comprehensive testing strategies.

7. **Q:** How do we prepare for a successful software release?
   **A:** Define release plans, conduct deployment rehearsals, and obtain necessary approvals for go-live.

8. **Q:** What practices ensure compliance with security and privacy standards?
   **A:** Conduct threat modeling, security reviews, and privacy impact assessments, ensuring adherence to standards like ISO/IEC 27001 and GDPR.

9. **Q:** How can we drive continuous improvement post-launch?
   **A:** Collect and analyze product usage data, conduct regular metrics reviews, and prioritize backlog based on evidence and user impact.

10. **Q:** What are the key roles and responsibilities in the software delivery lifecycle?
    **A:** The RACI matrix outlines responsibilities across phases, ensuring clarity in roles such as Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed.
